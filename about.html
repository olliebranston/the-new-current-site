About The New Current

The New Current is a personal website exploring the energy transition through writing, data, and reporting.

It is built around two halves: original thought and live intelligence.

What you will find here

  • Thought Pieces — longer-form essays and analysis on hot topics within the energy transition
  • Brain Dumps — short notes, quick reactions, and unfinished ideas (for further review)
  • Data — interactive charts and dashboards on markets, renewables, and emissions
  • Reporting — a curated view of news and developments across R&D, policy, and project finance

Why this site exists

The energy transition takes place across all aspects of societies, and is a breeding ground for hugely technical (and impressive) innovation. With such breadth and depth, not to mention pace of change, it can be difficult to keep track of what is actually going on.

The New Current is a place to think in public about the systems, markets, technologies, and policy developments shaping the energy transition.

It is also a place to combine commentary with evidence: written ideas alongside charts, monitoring tools, and reporting.

In progress

This site is being built step by step. Over time, it will grow to include more writing, more data tools, and more regular reporting.
