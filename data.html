Section One

Territorial versus consumption emissions

These charts compare different ways of counting UK emissions over the same 1996 to 2022 period. That matters because "UK emissions" can mean different things depending on whether you count emissions on a territorial basis, on a consumption basis, or on a per-person basis.

Territorial basis

UK territorial emissions

This chart tracks UK territorial greenhouse gas emissions, using the DESNZ territorial dataset and summing the published sector totals for each year. It is not the same thing as a consumption-based measure: it reflects emissions produced within the UK accounting boundary, rather than the emissions associated with everything the UK consumes.

Last updated: loading...

Consumption basis

UK consumption-based emissions

This chart tracks the UK's consumption-based emissions, often described as the UK's carbon footprint. It combines emissions from UK-produced goods and services consumed domestically, emissions embedded in imports, and the direct household heating and transport emissions included in the Defra series.

Last updated: loading...

Per person

UK consumption emissions per person

This chart takes the same consumption-based series shown to the left and divides it by the UK's mid-year population estimate for each year. It is a derived measure rather than a standalone official release, but it helps show how the average footprint has changed as both emissions and population have shifted over time.
